# Imagine-Cup-2026

## Overview

**Study Coach** is a learning assistant designed to help students practice, improve, and build confidence independently outside of tutoring sessions and class time.

Instead of directly giving away answers, the app guides students toward understanding by providing feedback, topic explanations, study tips, and additional practice questions based on what they get wrong. The goal is to support active learning and help students strengthen their understanding through guided practice.

This project was developed as a **minimum viable product (MVP)** for **Imagine Cup 2026**.

---

## Problem

Many students need more support outside of the classroom, but they do not always have immediate access to tutoring or extra guided practice.

Study Coach was created to address this by offering:
- additional feedback outside of tutoring sessions
- extra exercises for continued practice
- topic explanations to reinforce understanding
- a more interactive and supportive learning experience

---

## Features

- **Study Tips**  
  Provides learning tips to help students approach problems more effectively.

- **Question Generation**  
  Offers additional practice questions based on student needs.

- **Topic Explanations**  
  Gives explanations to help students better understand important concepts.

- **Guided Learning Support**  
  Encourages students to work toward answers instead of simply revealing them directly.

---

## Tech Stack

- **Python**
- **Streamlit**
- **JSON**

---

## How It Works

The application uses **JSON** to store topic definitions and structured responses. These definitions help simulate AI-style guidance by allowing the app to respond with relevant explanations, support, and follow-up learning content.

The app is designed as a **learning assistant** rather than a direct answer generator, with the purpose of helping students practice and improve their problem-solving process.

## Why Streamlit

I chose **Streamlit** because it allowed me to quickly build an interactive web-based MVP using Python. Since the project had limited development time, Streamlit was an efficient way to create a functional prototype and focus on the core learning experience.

---

## Challenges

One of the most challenging parts of the project was creating the **definitions and responses** used in the JSON structure. It was important to make sure the content was correct, useful, and aligned with the goal of guiding students without directly giving them the final answer.
